▾
⚡ What is a Calorie?
A calorie is a unit of energy. Your body uses calories to breathe, think, move, and repair. 1 kcal = energy to raise 1kg of water by 1°C. Food gives you calories. Movement spends them.
2,000avg daily need (women)
2,500avg daily need (men)
3,500kcal = 1 lb of fat
1
Find your TDEE 🎯
Total Daily Energy Expenditure = BMR × activity multiplier. Sedentary ×1.2, lightly active ×1.375, moderately active ×1.55.
✓
2
Read a food label 🏷️
Pick any food. Find: serving size, calories per serving, protein/carbs/fat. Multiply by servings you actually eat.
⏱ 3 mins
✓
3
Log yesterday's meals 📋
Estimate breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks. Use: protein ~4 kcal/g, carbs ~4 kcal/g, fat ~9 kcal/g.
⏱ 5 mins
✓
4
Set your target 🎯
Lose weight: TDEE −300 to −500 kcal. Maintain: eat at TDEE. Build muscle: TDEE +200 to +300 kcal.
✓
5
Track for one day 📱
Use a notes app or MyFitnessPal. Log every meal today. No judgment — just awareness.
⏱ all day

Portion Control Mastery 🍽️
Eat right without counting every calorie · +380 XP
▾
✋ Your Hand is Your Scale
You always have a measuring tool with you. Palm = protein serving. Fist = carbs. Thumb = fats. Cupped hand = veggies. Research shows visual cues reduce overeating by up to 30% without any tracking apps.
30%less eaten with smaller plates
20mindelay before fullness signal
½ plateshould be vegetables
1
Learn the hand method ✋
1 palm = ~100g protein (chicken, fish, tofu). 1 cupped fist = ~1 cup carbs (rice, pasta). 1 thumb = ~1 tbsp fat (oil, nut butter). Your hand scales with your body size.
✓
2
The plate method 🥗
Divide your plate: ½ non-starchy veg, ¼ lean protein, ¼ complex carbs. Add a thumb of healthy fat. This naturally hits ~500–600 kcal per meal without counting anything.
⏱ Build one plate today
✓
3
Slow eating practice 🐢
Put your fork down between bites. Chew 20 times. Satiety hormones (leptin/ghrelin) take 20 minutes to signal fullness. Eating too fast causes consistent overeating of 100–300 kcal.
⏱ 20 mins for next meal
✓
4
Shrink the vessel 🥣
Switching from a 30cm to 25cm plate reduces intake by ~22% (Cornell Food Lab). Use taller narrower glasses for juice, wider shallower ones for water. Environmental design beats willpower.
✓
5
Hunger vs appetite check 🔍
Before eating, rate true hunger 1–10. Under 4? Wait 15 minutes and drink a glass of water. You may be bored, stressed, or slightly dehydrated — not genuinely hungry.
⏱ Try before every meal today
✓

Sleep & Recovery Science 😴
The most underrated performance tool · +400 XP
▾
🌙 Sleep = Gains
90% of muscle repair happens during deep sleep. Poor sleep raises cortisol (stores fat), lowers testosterone (loses muscle), impairs insulin sensitivity, and increases hunger hormones by 24%. 8 hours isn't lazy — it's training.
7–9hoptimal for adults
90 minper sleep cycle
+24%hunger hormones when sleep-deprived
1
Set a consistent wake time ⏰
Your circadian rhythm is anchored by wake time, not bedtime. Same wake time 7 days a week is the single most impactful sleep change you can make.
✓
2
Cold, dark, quiet room 🌡️
Optimal sleep temp: 16–19°C. Darkness triggers melatonin. Silence or white noise prevents micro-arousals. Blackout blinds + slightly cracked window = elite sleep setup.
✓
3
Wind-down protocol 🌙
60 mins before bed: no screens (or blue light glasses), dim lights, no intense exercise, limit alcohol (disrupts deep sleep even in small amounts).
⏱ plan your 60-min wind-down
✓
4
Strategic napping ⚡
A 20-minute nap (set alarm for 25 mins) between 1–3pm improves alertness by 34% and motor learning. Longer than 30 mins causes sleep inertia. Nap before 3pm to avoid disrupting night sleep.
✓
5
Track your sleep debt 📊
Sleep debt is cumulative. Missing 2h/night for 5 days = same impairment as missing 1 full night. Recovery: 2 extra hours/night for a week clears most debt.

Skin Science 101 ✨
Understand your skin to actually fix it · +350 XP
▾
🔬 Your Skin is an Organ
Skin is the body's largest organ — 1.5–2m² of it. It has 3 layers: epidermis (outer barrier), dermis (collagen, elastin, sweat glands), hypodermis (fat insulation). Most "miracle" products only reach the epidermis. True skin health comes from hydration, sleep, nutrition and SPF — not price tags.
28 daysskin cell turnover cycle
SPF 30+prevents 97% of UVB rays
70%of ageing is sun damage
1
Identify your skin type 🔍
Wash face, wait 30 mins, no product. Oily all over = oily. Flaky + tight = dry. Oily T-zone + dry cheeks = combination. Easily red/reactive = sensitive. Normal = balanced. Most people misidentify.
✓
2
The 3-step non-negotiables 🌿
Cleanse (remove grime, excess oil — twice daily). Moisturise (every skin type, even oily — dehydration causes overproduction). SPF (every morning, rain or shine — this is the #1 anti-ageing product). Everything else is optional.
⏱ Start tonight with these 3
✓
3
Active ingredients decoded 🧪
Retinol — speeds cell turnover, reduces lines (use PM, start weekly). Vitamin C — brightens, antioxidant (AM). Niacinamide — reduces pores, redness (any time). Hyaluronic acid — hydrates all types (damp skin). AHAs/BHAs — exfoliate (2–3× per week max).
✓
4
Diet-skin connection 🥗
High glycaemic foods (sugar, white bread) spike insulin and trigger inflammation and acne. Omega-3s (salmon, walnuts, flaxseed) reduce inflammation. Vitamin C foods build collagen. 8 glasses of water improves skin elasticity measurably in 4 weeks.
✓
5
Build your AM/PM routine 🌅
AM: Cleanse → Vitamin C → Moisturiser → SPF. PM: Double cleanse (oil then water) → Retinol or treatment → Moisturiser. Less is more — a consistent simple routine beats an inconsistent complex one.
✓

🧬 Your Body Detoxes Itself
Your liver, kidneys, lymphatic system and gut already perform continuous detoxification. No juice cleanse can outperform them. Real "detoxing" means removing what burdens these systems — processed food, alcohol, stress, poor sleep — while providing what supports them: water, fibre, antioxidants, movement.
1.5Lblood filtered by liver per minute
30gdaily fibre goal for gut detox
7–9hrsleep = brain's glymphatic clean
1
Morning liver flush 🌅
Warm water + juice of half a lemon first thing. Stimulates bile production, aids liver enzyme activity, kickstarts digestion. Add a pinch of turmeric for extra anti-inflammatory effect. Do this before coffee for 7 days.
✓
2
Eliminate the Big 3 for 7 days 🚫
Remove: Alcohol (taxing on liver phase 1 & 2 detox), Refined sugar (inflammatory, feeds bad gut bacteria), Ultra-processed food (emulsifiers disrupt gut lining). Replace with whole foods. 7 days is enough to notice energy and skin changes.
✓
3
Lymphatic drainage movement 🏃
Unlike the circulatory system, the lymphatic system has no pump — it relies on movement. 20 mins of walking, rebounding (mini trampoline), or dry brushing stimulates lymph flow and reduces puffiness. Rebounding is 3× more effective than walking for lymph.
⏱ 20 mins movement now
✓
4
Gut fibre protocol 🌱
Fibre feeds beneficial gut bacteria that produce short-chain fatty acids — key for immunity, mood (90% of serotonin is made in the gut), and metabolic health. Aim for 30 different plant foods per week. Each one is a different fibre type.
✓
5
Sweat therapy 🧖
Sweating expels trace heavy metals, BPA, and phthalates through skin. Sauna (15–20 min at 80°C), hot yoga, or vigorous exercise all work. Shower immediately after — toxins reabsorb if left on skin. 3× per week is optimal for detox benefit.

6
Digital & stress detox 🔕
Chronic stress floods your body with cortisol, impairing liver detox pathways and gut permeability. A daily 30-min digital detox (no screens) + one phone-free morning per week measurably lowers cortisol within 2 weeks.

At-Home Spa Ritual 🛁
Evidence-based relaxation that actually restores · +320 XP
▾
🧠 Why Rituals Work
Spa rituals trigger the parasympathetic nervous system — "rest and digest." Warm water raises body temperature, then the drop after causes deep sleep. Magnesium from Epsom salts absorbs transdermally. Aromatherapy (lavender) reduces cortisol by 24% in clinical trials. These aren't luxuries — they're biological levers.
24%cortisol reduction (lavender)
60°Coptimal sauna temperature
90minbefore bed: ideal bath time
1
Set the environment 🕯️
Dim lights (candlelight or 2700K warm bulbs). Play binaural beats or lo-fi music at low volume. Remove phone from room. Prepare a warm drink (chamomile, ashwagandha latte). Your nervous system responds to environmental cues before you even begin.
✓
2
Epsom salt bath protocol 🛁
2 cups Epsom salt (magnesium sulphate) in warm (not hot) water. Soak 20 minutes minimum. Add 5 drops lavender essential oil. Magnesium deficiency affects 50% of adults — it's absorbed through skin and reduces muscle tension and anxiety.
⏱ 20+ minute soak
✓
3
Face mask + gua sha 💆
Clay mask (kaolin/bentonite) for oily/acne-prone skin. Hydrating mask (hyaluronic, aloe) for dry/sensitive. While mask sets, use a gua sha tool with facial oil — it promotes lymphatic drainage and reduces puffiness by 30–40% with consistent use.
✓
4
Body exfoliation + oil ritual 🫒
Dry brush before shower (upward strokes toward heart). Follow with a body scrub (sugar + coconut oil). After shower, apply body oil to damp skin within 60 seconds — this seals in moisture. Skin feels completely different within 2 weeks.
⏱ 15 mins full ritual
✓
5
Schedule your weekly ritual 📅
Even 30 minutes once a week creates measurable HRV (heart rate variability) improvement over 4 weeks. Block the time now. Treat it like any other health appointment — it isn't self-indulgent, it's maintenance.

Hair & Scalp Health 💇
Feed your follicles from inside and out · +330 XP
▾
🧬 Hair is Dead — Scalp is Alive
Hair strands are dead keratin. You cannot repair split ends with product — you can only seal them temporarily. Healthy hair grows from a healthy scalp — good blood flow, balanced sebum, low inflammation. Nutrition matters enormously: iron deficiency is the leading cause of hair loss in women.
0.5inavg hair growth per month
100hairs shed daily is normal
Iron#1 nutrient for hair loss
1
Scalp massage protocol 👐
4 minutes of scalp massage daily increases hair thickness by 19% over 24 weeks (study: ePlasty). Use fingertips (not nails) in circular motions. Add rosemary oil — clinically proven as effective as minoxidil for hair growth with no side effects.
✓
2
The protein-moisture balance 💧
Hair needs both protein (keratin structure) and moisture (elasticity). Too much protein = brittle. Too much moisture = limp. Signs of protein lack: stretches and snaps. Signs of moisture lack: no stretch, just breaks. Alternate protein mask / moisture mask weekly.
✓
3
Nutrition for hair growth 🥦
Key nutrients: Iron (red meat, lentils, spinach), Biotin (eggs, almonds), Zinc (pumpkin seeds, beef), Vitamin D (sun, salmon, supplements), Silica (cucumber, oats). Hair is a low-priority tissue — it starves first when nutrition is poor.
✓
4
Heat & damage protocol 🔥
Always use heat protectant before any tool. Max temperature: 180°C fine hair, 200°C thick/coarse. Air dry 80% before heat styling. Sleep on silk/satin pillowcase — reduces friction and breakage by up to 43% compared to cotton.
✓

Ballet & Grace
Posture, poise, and mindful precision
All levelsPosture & focus20 min
▾
1
The 5 positions of the feet 👣
1st: heels together, toes out. 2nd: feet shoulder-width, toes out. 3rd: one foot midway in front. 4th: one foot forward, gap between. 5th: front foot heel touching back toe.
○
2
Plié — the foundation 🦢
In 2nd position: bend knees over toes, spine tall, shoulders relaxed. Demi-plié (small bend). Grand plié (full bend). 8 repetitions each. This builds leg strength and attention.
○
3
Port de bras — arm flow 🌊
Arms in 1st (oval in front), 2nd (out to sides), 5th (overhead). Move between them slowly to music. Port de bras means "carriage of the arms" — graceful, not mechanical.
○
4
Relevé and balance 🕊️
Rise onto the balls of your feet (relevé). Hold for 5 seconds. Lower with control. Do 10 reps. This builds ankle strength and forces present-moment focus — you cannot balance while distracted.
○
5
Révérence — closing bow 🙇
The traditional ballet closing: step to the side, cross one foot behind, lower into a graceful curtsy or bow. This ritual of gratitude closes your body and your mind for the session.
